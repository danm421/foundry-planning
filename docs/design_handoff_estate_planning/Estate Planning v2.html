v2 revision notes

  • Canvas now reads as a timeline, not a Kanban board. The center column is a vertical death-sequence spine: Today → First death (taxes + marital inheritance) → Combined estate → Second death (taxes + to heirs) → Beneficiary split. Reference palette used literally — slate-teal for estate blocks, muted rust for tax/expenses band, muted blue for inheritance, olive for heirs.
  • Clients are the draggable source, not a flat asset rail. Left column collapses to two large cards (Tom / Linda). Expanding reveals their outright-owned assets (draggable) and ½-attributed joint assets (read-only, marked with ⇋). This matches how an advisor thinks in an estate meeting — “what does Tom own that we can move?” — and cuts the visible asset count roughly in half at rest.
  • Out of Estate is a list of trust cards. Each trust is a collapsed row with name, type tag, grantor, value, and asset count. Click to expand and see held assets, drop targets, exemption usage, and remove affordances. A persistent summary panel at the bottom shows aggregate metrics (held outside, exemption used/remaining, tax saved).
  • The big takeaway lives at the bottom of the spine. Two totals cards — Total Taxes & Expenses (red) and Total to Heirs (teal) — echo the reference’s closing punctuation. The rest of the canvas is quieter: reduced padding, no per-row category labels in the default view, fewer competing chips.
  • Drag behavior scoped narrowly. Only outright-owned assets inside an expanded client card are draggable; joint assets are locked and require an allocation step first. Valid drop targets are trust cards in the right column — the center spine is read-only output.
