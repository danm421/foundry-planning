Design rationale — 5 notes

  • Muted teal as the single accent. One color (#3FB8AF) carries all interactive and affirmative meaning — drop targets, preview deltas, proposed arrangements, primary CTAs. Semantic red and warm amber are reserved for exposure and warnings; positive deltas use a desaturated sage so teal stays special. No rainbow, no gradient sprawl.
  • Three surface tiers + tight borders, not shadows. #0A0C10 base → #12151C panel → #1A1E27 card, separated by 1px lines at #21–2A. No elevated drop shadows on data; only the dragging ghost and toast get shadow. This matches the Bloomberg-terminal density goal and lets numbers be the loudest thing on screen.
  • Drag affordance is a numbers-first preview, not a visual celebration. Valid trusts get a soft teal border; the hot one gets a pulsing accent strip and a mirrored “+$4.20M” holding inside. The real payload is the sidebar flipping to preview mode — current value struck through, proposed value bumped up in accent, explicit deltas (+$320K, −1.6pp). The advisor is a numbers person; we lead with numbers.
  • Sidebar is the persistent protagonist. It lives at 320px against every canvas state (default, drag, empty, even dimmed during modals) so the advisor can run meetings without ever looking away from impact. In the empty state it becomes a warning ($520K exposure) to create narrative urgency; during drag it becomes the preview surface; after commit it returns to factual.
  • Tabular numbers everywhere, geometric type for labels. Inter for UI, JetBrains Mono for every dollar figure, percentage, and ratio. Numbers of different magnitudes line up at the decimal across rows — in the before/after table, the Sankey node labels, the per-asset table. That alignment is what separates “hedge-fund terminal” from “SaaS report.”
