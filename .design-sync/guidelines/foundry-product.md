# Foundry product & positioning

What Foundry Planning is, who it's for, and what to say about it. Use this on
**marketing and product surfaces** — landing pages, heroes, feature sections,
pricing, onboarding, empty states — anywhere the design has to communicate the
product, not just render the app. It rides on top of the voice and copy rules in
[foundry-brand.md](./foundry-brand.md); nothing here overrides those.

## Product

Foundry Planning is financial planning software built for how advisors actually
work — not just the math, the whole workflow. Underneath is a deep cash-flow
engine (year-by-year income, spending, taxes, withdrawals, Monte Carlo, Social
Security, estate). But "cash-flow planning software" is the category, not the
pitch — plenty of tools do the math. The reason advisors switch is everything
around it: they get client data in a fraction of the time, model live on screen
in the meeting, and hand the client a plan they can actually read.

## What makes it different — lead with these

The engine is table stakes. On any surface that has to sell or explain Foundry,
lead with the workflow, not the math.

- **Fast data entry.** A client's full financial picture goes in in a fraction
  of the time other software demands — import and extract instead of typing for
  an afternoon.
- **A live, on-screen presentation tool.** Change an assumption in front of the
  client and watch the results update on screen. Run the what-if in the meeting,
  not the night before.
- **Plans clients actually read.** Foundry condenses what would be a 100-page
  plan into a focused 5–10 page presentation the client follows without
  translation.
- **AI in the right spots.** AI handles the busywork — data extraction, first
  drafts, surfacing what matters — and stays out of where the numbers and the
  advisor's judgment have to be exact.
- **One tool, not a stack.** Cash flow, tax planning (withdrawal-year drag,
  bracket management, Roth conversions), Monte Carlo, Social Security timing, and
  estate — together, no exports, no second app.

## Who it's for

Independent financial advisors, RIAs, and wealth managers who build detailed
plans and present them to clients across the table — and who lose hours to slow
data entry and to plans clients never read.

## Audience pains — what a surface should speak to

- Data entry that eats an afternoon before the real work starts.
- Plans so long (100+ pages) the client skims page 3 and asks what it means.
- Can't model a client's "what if" live — it's "let me run that and get back to
  you."
- Legacy tools (eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital) are slow, dense, and
  expensive.
- A "92% success" score means little to a client after a down year.

## Voice

Full contract in [foundry-brand.md](./foundry-brand.md). The non-negotiables:
direct, specific, confident, quiet. Numbers, not adjectives ("condense 100 pages
to 8" beats "simplified"). Lead with the person; the number lands second. No
exclamation points. Never: leverage, unlock, empower, synergy, unleash, "we
believe." Test: would you say it aloud to a 70-year-old client across the table?

## Goal when designing

Awareness and authority among financial advisors — teach and earn trust first,
sell softly. On marketing and product surfaces: no hard pitch, no performance
promises, no advice aimed at end-investors. The design should make an advisor
think "this is built by someone who understands my day," not "this is a sales
page."
